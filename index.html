Hush in the Hollow

By: Rachel Diamond

About the Game

Hush in the Hollow is an interactive board game set in a magical forest overtaken by a goblin town. Move carefully to avoid alerting the goblin army as you journey through the ever-changing forest. Collect treasures, defeat enemies, and emerge as the victor of the forest.

How to Play

Objective

Be the first player to reach the maximum XP by exploring the forest, defeating enemies, collecting treasures, and surviving the dangers surrounding the goblin town.

Setup

  • Each player chooses a Character Class Card with unique abilities.
  • All players begin at the outer edge of the board.
  • Shuffle all decks: Enemy, Treasure, Resource, and Goblin Army.

Turn Structure

  1. Roll the dice.
  2. Move that number of tiles in any direction.
  3. Resolve the tile you land on.

Tile Encounters

Enemy Tiles

  • Draw an Enemy Card and roll the dice.
  • Success: Gain XP (listed on the card).
  • Failure: You are weakened next turn (lower rolls and limited abilities).
  • Damage Taken: Roll again and resolve again.

Treasure Tiles

  • Draw a Treasure Card and roll the dice.
  • Success: Gain the treasure (worth XP).
  • Failure: Treasure remains locked and returns to the bottom of the deck.
  • Destroyed: Treasure is lost permanently.

Resource Tiles

  • Draw a Resource Card:
    • Gold Coins: Used for trading or shops.
    • Potions: Boost dice rolls.
    • Armor / Weapons: Upgrade your character.
    • Sellable Resources: Trade for gold.

Player Combat

  • If two players land on the same tile, combat begins.
  • The initiating player sets a wager (XP or gold).
  • Both players roll the dice.
  • Highest roll wins. Items may be used to influence results.

Goblin Town Event

When a player moves too close to the goblin town, the Goblin Army is triggered. A Goblin Army Card is drawn, and all players may choose to join or decline the battle.

  • If you join:
    • Escaped: No consequence.
    • Injured: Debuffed next turn (lower rolls, no abilities).
    • Captured: You are jailed until you roll an 8 or higher.
  • If you decline:
    • No risk and no reward.

Core Mechanics

  • Exploration: Movement reveals and navigates the board.
  • Card Encounters: Most actions are resolved with cards and dice.
  • Resource Management: Collect and use items strategically.
  • Risk vs Reward: The closer to the goblin town, the greater the danger and reward.
  • Trigger Events: Actions may change the board and affect other players.

Winning the Game

The first player to reach the maximum XP wins the game.
