Hush in the Hollow

By: Rachel Diamond

About the Game

Hush in the Hollow is a stealth-based board game set in a magical forest that has been overtaken by a goblin town at its center. Players begin on the outskirts of the forest and must navigate inward, uncovering a hidden and ever-changing map as they move.

Along the way, players will collect treasures and encounter goblins—some of which can be fought or avoided depending on strategy. However, the real danger lies in the goblin army at the center of the forest. Every action risks alerting the growing forces within the goblin town, increasing tension as players move closer to their goal.

The game blends exploration, combat, and stealth, creating a balance between progression and caution in a constantly shifting environment revealed piece by piece.

How to Play

Players explore a hidden forest map, revealing tiles as they move. Each tile may contain treasures, goblins, or hazards. Players must balance exploration and stealth while avoiding alerting the goblin town at the center of the board.

Game Elements

The Board

Hidden modular board revealed through gameplay.

The Characters

Unique player roles with different abilities.

The Cards

Event and action cards that shape gameplay decisions.

How It Was Made

Fabrication

Laser cutting, 3D printing, and physical assembly were used to construct the modular board system.

Electronics

Neopixels, hall effect sensors, and servo motors detect movement and trigger game events.

Design Tools

Adobe Illustrator and Procreate were used for visual and card design.

Development Process

The game evolved through multiple iterations, prototypes, and playtests that refined mechanics and physical design.

Final Results

The final version combines hidden information, physical interaction, and strategic exploration into a cohesive board game experience.
